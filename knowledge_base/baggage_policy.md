# Wanderly Baggage Policy

## Carry-On Allowance
Most itineraries booked through Wanderly include one carry-on bag (max 22 x 14 x 9 in / 56 x 36 x 23 cm) and one personal item (purse, laptop bag, or small backpack) per traveler at no extra charge. Budget-carrier fares may restrict carry-ons to the personal item only — this is flagged on the flight option before booking.

## Checked Baggage Allowance
Standard economy fares include one checked bag up to 50 lbs / 23 kg. Premium economy, business, and first-class fares typically include two checked bags. Exact allowances vary by airline and route and are shown on each flight option Wanderly presents.

## Excess Baggage Fees
- Extra bag beyond the included allowance: $60-$150 per bag, per direction, depending on airline
- Overweight bag (51-70 lbs / 23-32 kg): $75-$200 per bag
- Oversized bag (linear dimensions over 62 in / 158 cm): $75-$200 per bag
- Bags over 70 lbs / 32 kg are generally refused for standard checked handling and must ship as cargo

Fees are collected directly by the airline at check-in, not by Wanderly.

## Special and Oversized Items
Sporting equipment (skis, surfboards, golf bags), musical instruments, and other oversized items usually require advance notice to the airline and may incur a flat special-item fee ($30-$150) instead of the standard excess-baggage rate. Wanderly recommends adding special items to a booking at least 48 hours before departure.

## Lost, Delayed, or Damaged Baggage
If checked baggage is lost, delayed, or damaged, report it to the airline's baggage service desk before leaving the airport to receive a file reference number. Wanderly's optional travel insurance plans (see the insurance policy) can reimburse reasonable expenses for delayed baggage and provide compensation for bags lost or damaged in transit; claims should be filed within 21 days of the incident.
