# Wanderly Cancellation Policy

## Standard Cancellation Window
Travelers may cancel any Wanderly-booked trip free of charge within 24 hours of booking, provided the departure date is at least 7 days away. After the 24-hour window, cancellation fees follow the tiered schedule below.

## Cancellation Fees by Timing
- 8+ days before departure: 10% of total trip cost
- 3-7 days before departure: 50% of total trip cost
- 0-2 days before departure or no-show: 100% of total trip cost (non-refundable)

Fees are calculated on the total package price, not on individual flight or hotel line items.

## Non-Refundable Bookings
Some fare classes and promotional hotel rates are marked "non-refundable" at checkout. These bookings cannot be cancelled for a refund at any time, though many airlines and hotels will allow date changes for a fee. Always check the fare rules shown before confirming a non-refundable booking.

## Airline and Hotel Partner Cancellation Rules
Wanderly's own cancellation fees are separate from, and in addition to, the cancellation terms set by the airline, hotel, or tour operator supplying the travel. When a partner's rules are stricter than Wanderly's schedule above, the stricter rule applies. Partner cancellation terms are always shown at checkout before you confirm a booking.

## Weather and Emergency Exceptions
Wanderly waives its own cancellation fee (partner fees may still apply) in cases of:
- A government travel advisory issued for the destination after booking
- Airport closure or flight cancellation by the airline for the travel dates
- A documented medical emergency affecting the traveler or an immediate family member

Supporting documentation is required and should be submitted within 14 days of the cancellation request.

## How to Cancel a Booking
Cancellations can be requested through the Wanderly chat assistant, by emailing support@wanderly.example, or through the "My Trips" section of the account dashboard. Refunds, when applicable, are issued to the original payment method within 5-10 business days.
